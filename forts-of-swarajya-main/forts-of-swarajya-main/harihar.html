-:HARIHAR FORT:-

Harihar Fort is a famous hill fort located in the Nashik district of Maharashtra. It is also known as Harshgad. The fort is built on a triangular rock hill. Harihar Fort is best known for its steep, rock-cut steps carved almost vertically. The fort stands at an elevation of about 3,676 feet above sea level. It was used as a watchtower to guard the trade route through Gonda Ghat. The climb to the fort is adventurous and challenging. At the top, there is a small temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. Water tanks and old structures can be seen on the fort. The fort offers breathtaking views of the surrounding Sahyadri ranges. Harihar Fort is popular among trekkers and adventure lovers. It reflects the strategic importance of hill forts in Maratha history.
