-:PURANDAR FORT:-

Purandar Fort is an important hill fort located near Pune in Maharashtra. The fort stands at an elevation of about 4,472 feet above sea level. It played a significant role in Maratha history during the reign of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. Purandar Fort is known for the historic Treaty of Purandar signed in 1665. The fort has two main sections: the upper fort (Balekilla) and the lower fort (Machhi). Strong fortification walls and bastions can be seen throughout the fort. There are several temples, including one dedicated to Lord Purandeshwar. The fort offers wide panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. Purandar Fort was an important military stronghold. It is also the birthplace of Sambhaji Maharaj, son of Shivaji Maharaj. Today, Purandar Fort is a popular trekking and heritage site. The fort reflects Maratha bravery and strategic planning.
